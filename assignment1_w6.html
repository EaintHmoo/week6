Architecture

Since 2002, with the development of Government Technological Colleges and Universities (GTCs and TUs) all over Myanmar, there become increasing number of students. The Department has produced over eight hundred promising Architects in 50 years’ duration (ie.1954-2004).

Our Mission

To educate and train students systematically in order to become engineers, specialists and researchers who- can effectively contribute to the building of a modern developed nation, have received high standard of education and training, can think rationally, approach and solve problems systematically, have the will and the ability to work for the good of the society, mankind and environment are conscious of civic duties and professional ethics.

Our Vision

It is envisaged that YTU will become a highly-prestigious technological center of excellence both in teaching and research with a strong commitment to systematically educate and train students of high caliber in order to become well-rounded, highly-qualified engineers, specialists with the ability to solve complex problems and researchers who can discover and innovate new things; all for the good of the society, mankind and the environment.

Our History

The Department of Architecture was founded in 1954 to conduct a four-year programme leading to the Degree of Bachelor of Architecture under the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Rangoon (Yangon). The first five graduates in architecture passed out into the profession in April 1958. In 1964, the Department was shifted to Rangoon Institute of Technology at Gyogone, Insein, which has been called Yangon Technological University (YTU) since 1998.

ABOUT YTU

The present Yangon Technological University (Y.T.U) began as the Department of Engineering in 1923 under the Rangoon (Yangon) University. The Engineering Department was situated downtown, on the Bogyoke Aung San street. B.Sc. (Engineering) program in Civil Engineering began in 1924.
